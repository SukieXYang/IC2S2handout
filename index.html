Climate Change Education: A Global Analysis of Curricular Policies and Implementation

Sukie Xiuqi Yang

PhD Candidate in Sociology and Demography, MA student in Statistics and Data Science, University of Pennsylvania

Research Overview

  • Investigates integration of climate change in national curricula among 160+ countries
  • Examines policy-practice gap in 50+ countries
  • Uses PISA 2018 data and UNESCO/MECCE Project data
  • Employs machine learning and multilevel modeling

Key Research Question

How do various factors influence the integration of climate change in national curricular policies? What accounts for the implementation of such policies at the school level?

Significance

This study addresses a critical gap in understanding climate change education policies and their implementation, contributing to efforts in countering climate denial and preparing future generations for climate action.

This is a part of my dissertation investigating the interplay between climate change and education
